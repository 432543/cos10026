Endangered Plants of Malaysia

C. asperum

Canarium asperum is a species of plant in the family Burseraceae. It is found in Brunei, Indonesia, Malaysia, Papua New Guinea, the Philippines, and the Solomon Islands. Contributed by Mark Kho.

D. costata

Dacryodes costata grows up to 45 metres (150 ft) tall with a trunk diameter of up to 45 centimetres (18 in). The grey-brown bark is smooth to flaky. The flowers are white. Contributed by John Chen

S. dacryodifolia

Santiria dacryodifolia is a species of plant in the family Burseraceae. It is a tree endemic to Borneo where it is confined to Sarawak. Contributed by Lisa Su.

P. grandifructus

Parastemon grandifructus is a tree in the family Chrysobalanaceae. The specific epithet grandifructus is from the Latin meaning "large fruit". Contributed by Lila Albert.

C. caudatum

Canarium caudatum is a tree in the family Burseraceae. The specific epithet caudatum is from the Latin meaning "tailed", referring to the tapering of the tree's leaflet. Contributed by Jack Frags

D. patentinervia

Dacryodes patentinervia is a tree in the family Burseraceae. It is endemic to Borneo, whereby it is known locally as sabal, sibut or seladah. Contributed by Michel Chan.
